Catskill Mountains, New York

Catskill Mountains are part of the breathtaking landscape found in Upstate New York. The area is known for it's diverse wildlife and hiking trails. It is one of the most popular vacation destination spot with New Yorkers.

Discover

Witness the lush wildlife

The New York State Department of Environment Conservation reports that the second largest population of New York state black bears resides in the Catskills, including most of the mountain ranges. Today, the black bear population numbers is about 2,000. Nearly extinct until the 1900s, coyotes have been reintroduced to the area. Two poisonous snakes are native to the Catskills: the timber rattlesnake and the northern copperhead.
